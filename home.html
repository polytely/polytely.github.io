Efficiency Oriented Energy Consumer Collaboration

We aim to develop the necessary infrastructure to perform scalable, adaptive learning of the consuming behaviour, efficiency improvements and generation capabilities of smart energy community members that collaborate to ensure quality of energy supply and environment preservation. The reason why we aim to explore the issue of consumer participation in the energy system transition by focusing on community formation is that while our behaviour in relation to energy consumption/prosumption and energy efficiency is affected by a series of interrelated factors, the creation and mobilization of communities around a shared goal, can affect a significant number of these factors.

The central premise is that by adopting a data-driven approach, one can build a descriptive model of the consumers’ behaviour, which can then be utilized for provision of guidance towards small, gradual and thus persistent changes in their consumption profiles. A need for algorithm adaptability stems from the fact that the characteristics of a community of consumers are dynamic both due to the improving energy efficiency and deployment of micro-generation technologies by its current members and due to the addition of new ones in the community.

Real time coordination of energy demand, storage and supply

If we focus only on load shifting, demand response is constrained by the fact that there is a limited potential for shifting load to a ‘better’ time. However, the main goal of demand response is making the best use of available resources, rather than load shifting or curtailment. In fact, demand response can be regarded as a solar and wind balancing mechanism. Optimal energy management strategies require controllability and predictability both on a local but also on an aggregate level, incorporating micro-generation, RES-E, energy storage systems and demand.

A similar approach used for the support of consumer coordination can help coordinate the capabilities of building blocks. The heterogeneity in capabilities makes coordination challenging, but it is necessary for achieving a high level of sufficiency or flexibility. The ability of micro-generation and storage enabled buildings to contribute – through aggregation – to demand response is a key enabler so that multiple homes can provide energy services when they are needed. Heat pumps can provide electricity demand reduction, even more when combined with thermal storage. Micro combined heat and power (Micro-CHP) can provide additional generation capacity at peak times and solar PV with storage can store electricity when generation is high but demand low, for use when system demand peaks either to export or for self consumption.
